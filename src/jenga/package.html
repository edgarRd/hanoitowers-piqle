Jenga is an game of skill : the player must take blocks out of a tower without making it fall down. But assuming that his skill is perfect, it turns out to be a Nim-like game, with a winning strategy for one of the players (depending of the highness of the tower).
This paper analyses the game, and describe the strategy.
The interest of the game in the perspective of Reinforcement Learning, is to see whether the algorithms found this strategy, and whether it is understandable (and quickly found !).
